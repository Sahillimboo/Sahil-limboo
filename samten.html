bhalay dhunga

The Bhaleydhunga Ropeway, also known as the Dhapper-Bhaleydhunga Ropeway, is located in Yangang, South Sikkim. Spanning approximately 3.5 kilometers, it holds the distinction of being India's longest ropeway. TIMES OF INDIA The journey begins at Dhapper, situated at an altitude of 1,800 meters, and ascends to the Bhaleydhunga cliff, reaching up to 3,500 meters. NATIVEPLANET This ropeway is recognized as one of the steepest in the world, featuring a 60-degree slope, and is considered among the top five most challenging ropeways globally. SIKKIM EXPRESS Equipped with 18 cabins, each accommodating eight passengers, the ropeway offers a 13-minute ride with panoramic views of the Himalayan range, dense forests, and the Maenam Wildlife Sanctuary. STAYVISTA Constructed by the French company Poma at a cost of ₹210 crore, the project was funded under the Prime Minister's Development for North East Region (PM-DevINE) initiative. NATIVEPLANET The ropeway was inaugurated on September 16, 2024, aiming to boost tourism in the region. TIMES OF INDIA The fare for a ride is ₹950 per person.
